How much do you think the workforce will need to update its core skills by 2030?

According to the WEF: 39% of workers' core skills are expected to change by 2030, and 59% of the global workforce will need retraining. This is a major shift — but it is about which skills matter, not necessarily that jobs disappear entirely.

Exposure, Risk & Opportunity

Some careers are more exposed to AI than others — but exposure is not the same as decline. Tap a career cluster to see where it sits and what that actually means.

Three things to keep separate: (1) Exposure — how many tasks AI can assist with. (2) Complementarity — how much human skill adds value alongside AI. (3) Demand growth — whether the sector is expanding or shrinking overall. A career can be highly exposed and still be a strong opportunity.

Key takeaways to remember

🧩

Tasks, not whole jobs

AI tends to reshape specific tasks within roles, not eliminate entire occupations at once. Human judgement, creativity, and relationships remain central in most work.

📈

Growth is everywhere

Growth is not only in technology. Care, education, construction, clean energy, and creative industries all show strong projected demand in UK Government data.

🛤️

Many routes in

Apprenticeships, T Levels, BTECs, FE colleges, degrees, and mixed routes all lead to valuable careers. 74.8% of workers in critical-demand roles require training below degree level.

🧠

Human skills still matter

Creative thinking, resilience, collaboration, communication, and leadership remain in high demand — alongside domain knowledge — even in the most AI-intensive sectors.
